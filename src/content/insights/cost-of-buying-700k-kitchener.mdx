When somebody tells me they're budgeting "$700,000 to buy a house in Kitchener," the first thing I do is open a calculator and walk them through what the real number is. The price is the headline. The closing costs are the fine print, and they add up faster than people expect.

## A real example

Let's say you're buying a $700,000 detached house in Kitchener with a 10% down payment ($70,000). You're a first-time buyer using a mortgage. Here's what actually hits your bank account between offer and move-in:

| Item | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Down payment | $70,000 |
| Ontario LTT (with first-time rebate) | $6,475 → after rebate $2,475 |
| Lawyer (real estate) | $1,500–$2,500 |
| Home inspection | $450–$700 |
| Title insurance | $300–$500 |
| CMHC insurance premium | $19,530 *(added to your mortgage, not paid upfront)* |
| Moving | $500–$2,500 |
| Utility hookups | $100–$300 |
| **Total cash out of pocket (excluding moving)** | **~$74,575–$76,275** |

The CMHC premium isn't cash — it gets rolled into your mortgage and you pay it over 25 years. But the $4,000+ in lawyer, inspection, and LTT is real cash on closing day. Plan for it.

## What surprises people

**The LTT calculation isn't a flat percent.** Ontario uses a bracketed system. On a $700k house: 0.5% on the first $55k, 1% on the next $195k, 1.5% on the next $150k, then 2% on the remaining $300k. That's $10,475 — but with the first-time buyer rebate (max $4,000), you pay $6,475. Toronto buyers pay this plus a *second* municipal LTT — we're spared that in KW.

**Inspection is non-negotiable.** Even on a new build. Every house I've walked through has had at least one thing the seller's photos didn't show. $500 is the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy.

**Title insurance is the boring expense that protects you from the boring disasters.** Things like an old lien nobody knew about. Pay it. Don't think about it again.

## What I'm not certain about

Closing costs creep with inflation. The numbers above are 2025-ish. By the time you read this, lawyer fees might be $1,800–$2,800, not $1,500–$2,500. Always quote your own lawyer in advance.

## What you should do with this information

Add roughly **1.5% of the purchase price** to your total budget as a safety buffer for closing-day cash. On a $700k house that's about $10,500 — enough to cover LTT, lawyer, inspection, title insurance, and a couple of move-in expenses without scrambling.
