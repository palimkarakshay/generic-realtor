# First-year content templates

A brand-new realtor's biggest content problem is having no past deals to point to. This document gives templates that don't rely on closed deals or testimonials.

## Goal

Ship one short insight post per month for the first year. 12 posts is enough to: rank for a handful of long-tail KW keywords, give the site real depth, and demonstrate that the realtor thinks before they speak.

## Tone

- Short. 400–700 words.
- Specific. Real numbers, real neighborhoods, real years.
- Honest. "I don't know yet" is allowed.
- No SEO stuffing. No "Top 10 reasons to..." listicles. No AI-generated filler.

## 12 evergreen post ideas for a brand-new KW realtor

Order is intentional — the early ones don't require deals or testimonials.

1. **"Why I got my real estate license (and why I'm not in a hurry)."** First-person, honest. Establishes who you are without needing a deal under your belt.

2. **"The cost of buying a $700,000 house in Kitchener, beyond the price."** LTT, lawyer, inspection, CMHC if applicable, moving. Real numbers. Useful for any first-time buyer.

3. **"What renting and buying actually cost, side by side, in Uptown Waterloo right now."** Pick a real condo, calc the buy-vs-rent math at current rates. Update annually.

4. **"How the Ontario Standard Lease actually works (and why landlords who don't use it should worry you)."** Tenant-facing. Builds trust with the rental side of your market.

5. **"What I look for when I walk into a 100-year-old KW house."** Drawing on your inspection-observation skills. Knob-and-tube wiring, foundation cracks, the smell of an old basement.

6. **"The first conversation, and what's actually in it."** Demystifies what working with a realtor early is like. No commitment, no contract, no pressure.

7. **"Why I tell some buyers to wait."** Counterintuitive. Builds the "honest realtor" positioning. Talk about specific scenarios where waiting is the right call (job uncertain, market frothy, savings short).

8. **"A quick LTT math primer for Ontario first-time buyers."** Ties to the calculator on the buy page. Up-to-date with current rebates.

9. **"What a CMA actually is, and what you should expect."** Demystifies the seller-side process before anyone hires you.

10. **"Three KW neighborhoods I've changed my mind about."** Personal, specific, slightly contrarian. The kind of post that gets read because it's not generic.

11. **"What happened when I sat in on three KW open houses in one weekend."** Pre-licensing or early-licensing observation post. Field notes.

12. **"My one-year reflection: what I got right and wrong."** End of year 1. Career-arc honesty. Goes into the long-term portfolio.

## Per-post structure template

```
# [Specific, declarative title — not a question]

[1-sentence hook. State the point of the post immediately.]

[2–4 paragraphs. Real numbers, real examples, real names where possible.]

[1 paragraph: what I'm still uncertain about. Permission to not know everything.]

[1 paragraph: what you should do with this information. Specific, actionable.]

[Closing line. Quiet. Don't end with a CTA.]
```

## What NOT to publish

- "Top 10" listicles (everyone does this; you stand out by not).
- AI-generated content. Search engines and humans both detect it.
- Anything market-prediction-flavored ("KW prices will rise 8% in 2026"). Pure liability.
- Testimonials you wrote yourself. Don't.
- Listings dressed up as "case studies" with no insight.

## When to publish

- 1 post on launch day (the "Why I got licensed" one)
- 1 post per month thereafter
- Backdate the launch post to a believable date so it doesn't look like the site started yesterday

## Reusing posts

After 6 months, the best posts get:
- Posted to LinkedIn (your professional channel)
- Posted to Instagram as carousels (the photo + a 200-word excerpt)
- Sent as a "monthly thoughts" email to the realtor's small but growing list (when you have one)

The post is the asset. Everything else is distribution.
